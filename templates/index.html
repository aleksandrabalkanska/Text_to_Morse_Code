Welcome to the Morse Code Converter

Morse code was developed in the early 1830s by Samuel Morse and Alfred Vail as a means of long-distance communication using telegraph systems.

It uses a combination of dots and dashes to represent letters and numbers, with shorter signals denoting dots and longer signals denoting dashes.

Morse code played a crucial role in maritime and military communication for many years and has had a lasting impact on the development of telecommunications.
